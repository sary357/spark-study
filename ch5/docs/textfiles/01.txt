Authorities continue to update casualty numbers across Ukraine after Russia launched its biggest air attack since its full-scale invasion of Ukraine began in February 2022.

So far, 31 people have been reported killed, according to regional and national officials:

Kyiv: Nine killed and 24 injured, according to State Emergency Service of Ukraine
Zaporizhzhia: Eight killed and 13 injured, according to Yuriy Malashko, head of Zaporizhzhia region military administration
Dnipropetrovsk: Six killed and 38 injured, according to Serhii Lysak, head of Dnipropetrovsk region military administration 
Odesa: Four killed and 24 injured, according to Mayor Gennadiy Trukhanov
Kharkiv: Three killed and 13 injured, according to Oleh Syniehubov, the head of the regional military administration
Lviv: One killed and 30 injured, according to Maksym Kozytskyi, the head of Lviv region military administration 
Cherkasy: Eight injured in Smila, according to Ihor Taburets, head of Cherkasy region military administration 
Sumy: Two injured in Konotop, according to the Sumy regional prosecutor's office
